# Stewardship Transition Plan

## Mission

Ensure Vindicate remains operable, governable, and auditable if the founding team fully exits.

## Founder Exit Strategy

### Planned transition windows

- Window A (2026-2029): founders retain execution authority with growing delegation obligations.
- Window B (2030-2034): founders shift to advisory-only roles for core protocol matters.
- Window C (2035 onward): founders hold no privileged operational authority.

### Exit controls

- Mandatory transfer of role keys and legal authorities on scheduled milestones.
- Public disclosure of retained vs surrendered powers.
- Independent stewardship audit before each transition window closes.

## Foundation and Entity Structure

- Protocol Foundation: safeguards constitutional charter, standards continuity, and public-interest mission.
- Operating Entities: deliver enterprise services under jurisdiction-specific legal obligations.
- Stewardship Council: multi-stakeholder body overseeing continuity, not day-to-day execution.

## Multi-Generational Governance Design

- Term limits for continuity-critical governance roles.
- Rotating committees for technical, economic, and policy stewardship.
- Apprenticeship pipeline for future maintainers and governance delegates.
- Cross-generational representation targets in governance participation.

## Knowledge Preservation Plan

- Single source of truth documentation with versioned governance and technical decisions.
- Mandatory architecture decision records for all protocol-critical changes.
- Quarterly runbook validation drills and update requirements.
- Archive policy for historical rationale, incident lessons, and migration records.

## Documentation Continuity Model

- Minimum documentation standards enforced for every major release.
- Deprecation and migration guides required before enabling breaking changes.
- Stewardship handoff bundles for each critical subsystem.
- Documentation ownership matrix with backup maintainers per domain.

## Anti-Bottleneck Controls

| Bottleneck Risk | Prevention Mechanism |
| --- | --- |
| Single maintainer dependency | Two-deep ownership and rotation requirements |
| Undocumented operational knowledge | Runbook and incident record quality gates |
| Founder-centric governance trust | Multi-chamber approval and independent oversight |
| Legal-entity dependency in one region | Multi-jurisdiction entity and trustee distribution |

## Continuity Drills

- Annual "founder-offline" governance and operations exercise.
- Semiannual key-person absence simulation for technical maintenance roles.
- Cross-region legal disruption simulation for entity continuity readiness.

## Success Criteria

- Protocol remains fully functional and governable for 12+ months without founder intervention.
- No critical subsystem has single-person documentation or operational ownership.
- Stewardship transitions complete with no emergency governance exceptions.
