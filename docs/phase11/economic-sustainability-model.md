# Economic Sustainability Model (20-Year)

## Economic Objective

Maintain uninterrupted verification continuity through at least two severe market contractions without relying on speculative token appreciation.

## Treasury Longevity Architecture

Treasury is segmented into four policy buckets:

- Continuity Reserve: funds core protocol operation and security minimums.
- Security Reserve: incident response, audits, and emergency infrastructure replacement.
- Ecosystem Investment: grants, integrations, and adoption programs.
- Strategic Optionality: research, migration programs, and geopolitical contingency actions.

## 20-Year Projection Framework

### Scenario assumptions

- Baseline annual real cost reduction in core infra: 2-4% through automation and protocol maturity.
- Two bear-market windows over 20 years, each lasting 24-36 months.
- Non-token revenues grow as a share of total treasury inflows over time.

### Projection model by era

| Era | Timeline | Treasury Posture | Survival Target |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| E1 | 2026-2030 | Build reserves and reduce discretionary burn volatility | Maintain minimum 60 months runway in continuity + security reserves |
| E2 | 2031-2035 | Shift toward service-backed revenue dominance | Keep at least 70% of core opex covered by non-speculative revenue |
| E3 | 2036-2040 | Finance cryptographic and governance migrations without reserve depletion | Maintain 84 months low-cost survival runway |
| E4 | 2041-2046 | Operate mature maintenance and renewal cycles | Preserve perpetual maintenance fund with annual replenishment rules |

## Revenue Diversification Strategy

- Enterprise verification subscriptions and SLA tiers.
- Institutional issuance and compliance tooling fees.
- Standards conformance and interoperability certification services.
- Managed regional gateway services for regulated deployments.
- Treasury yield from conservative, policy-approved instruments where legally permissible.

## Bear-Market Survival Strategy

- Trigger-based budget contraction tied to reserve runway thresholds.
- Automatic pause of non-essential grant disbursements when runway falls below policy floor.
- Protection of security, verification availability, and governance continuity budgets.
- Pre-negotiated vendor reduction clauses and decentralized fallback operators.

## Low-Cost Maintenance Mode

### Activation criteria

- Continuity reserve runway below 36 months.
- Material decline in recurring service revenue.
- Systemic market or regulatory stress conditions.

### Operating profile

- Freeze non-critical feature development.
- Maintain only core verification, revocation, and integrity services.
- Shift to slower but cheaper infrastructure profiles where safe.
- Prioritize asynchronous governance and operational workflows.

## Decentralized Maintenance Incentives

- Long-term maintainer grants tied to measurable continuity outcomes.
- Bug bounty and recovery bounty pools funded from security reserve policy.
- Reputation and governance weight benefits for verifiable maintenance contributions.
- Regional maintenance cooperatives for infrastructure redundancy.

## Economic Health Metrics

- Months of continuity runway at current burn and maintenance burn.
- Share of revenue from non-speculative utility sources.
- Reserve coverage ratio for incident and migration obligations.
- Cost per million verifications over time.
- Maintenance contributor retention rate.

## Economic Failure Prevention Rules

- Do not fund recurring core operations from one-time speculative gains.
- Do not increase discretionary commitments during peak volatility periods.
- Do not approve major expansion proposals without stress-tested 5-year cost coverage.
