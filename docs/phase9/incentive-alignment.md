# Incentive Alignment by Participant

## Institutions

### Why join
- Reduced fraud exposure and faster verification acceptance by employers.
- Governance participation rights via stake and protocol reputation.
- Access to ecosystem integration grants for onboarding systems.

### Why stay
- Reputation compounding over time (public trust score and badge tiers).
- Lower integration costs through protocol SDK and standard APIs.
- Stronger employer-side demand for verified credentials.

### Why not cheat
- Issuer/institution bonds at risk under slashing process.
- Public revocation and fraud history damage long-run trust score.
- Future issuance privilege removal through governance action.

## Issuers

- Economic stake: minimum bond requirement and slashable collateral.
- Reputation stake: score reductions impact governance power and approval probability.
- Positive behavior unlocks higher trust and lower review friction.

## Students

- Ownership: portable, verifiable records independent of institution lock-in.
- Portability incentives: reusable cross-platform verification across networks.
- Fraud resilience: immutable proof with revocation transparency.

## Verifiers

- Fast verification path via API/SDK and chain-agnostic anchors.
- Optional rewards through `VindicateVerificationRewards` for verified contribution.
- Lower compliance burden via standardized audit-compatible checks.

## Developers

- SDK adoption incentives through grants and usage-based reward bands.
- Protocol-native API key tiers and ecosystem integration funding.
- Public attribution and trust scores for verified open-source contributions.
